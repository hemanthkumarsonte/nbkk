About

Nayak Budokan Karate Academy (NBKA)

Nayak Budokan Karate Academy (NBKA) was founded by the visionary P. Ganesh Nayak along with his loyal team, under the divine blessings and mentorship of Grand Master C. Hanumanth Rao. With an unwavering passion for martial arts and an indomitable spirit to empower individuals, P. Ganesh Nayak embarked on this journey to create a legacy in karate and self-defense.

The name Nayak Budokan Karate Academy was a heartfelt gesture by Grand Master C. Hanumanth Rao, who named the academy after his cherished student as a mark of love, respect, and recognition for P. Ganesh Nayak’s dedication to the art of karate. Their teacher-student bond is a testament to the values of trust, loyalty, and mutual admiration relationship that continues to inspire generations of martial artists.

Affiliation with Budokan Karate-Do International

NBKA is proudly affiliated with Budokan Karate-Do International, a globally recognized organization that has been instrumental in spreading the art and philosophy of karate across the world. Established in 1966 by Grand Master Chew Choo Soot in Malaysia, Budokan Karate-Do International combines the essence of traditional martial arts with a modern approach to discipline and self-defense.

Grand Master Chew Choo Soot, a master of multiple martial arts forms, founded Budokan Karate-Do with the vision of creating a comprehensive style that would blend the best techniques and philosophies of various disciplines. His dedication laid the groundwork for what has become a worldwide movement, inspiring millions to embrace the transformative power of karate.

In 1978, Budokan Karate-Do found its way to India through the efforts of Grand Master C. Hanumanth Rao, who was personally mentored by Chew Choo Soot. Recognizing Hanumanth Rao’s exceptional skill and leadership qualities, Grand Master Chew entrusted him with the responsibility of nurturing and expanding the Budokan legacy in India. With this responsibility, Hanumanth Rao established a strong foundation for Budokan Karate-Do in India, training thousands of students and ensuring its teachings reached every corner of the country.

Grand Master C. Hanumanth Rao’s efforts not only preserved the principles of Budokan Karate-Do but also elevated its stature on the global stage. His unwavering dedication has inspired numerous instructors and students, including P. Ganesh Nayak, who continues to carry forward this prestigious legacy through NBKA.

Budokan Karate-Do International remains a unifying platform for martial artists, with its presence in countries worldwide. NBKA’s affiliation with this esteemed organization ensures that its students receive training that aligns with international standards, preparing them to excel not only in competitions but also in life.

A Legacy of Inspiration

P. Ganesh Nayak’s journey is one of relentless dedication and inspiration. His commitment to spreading the art of karate and self-defense is unwavering. He dreams of reaching every corner of society, ensuring that more people benefit from the life-changing impact of martial arts. His words echo with wisdom and motivation: “Karate is not just about fighting; it is about facing life with courage, focus, and determination. It is about building a strong mind and a fearless spirit.”
